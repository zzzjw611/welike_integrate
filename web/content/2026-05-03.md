# Case Study: How Gong Used AI-Powered Email Scoring to Boost Demo Bookings by 40%

## Background

Gong is a leading revenue intelligence platform that captures and analyzes customer interactions—such as sales calls, emails, and meetings—to provide actionable insights for sales teams. Founded in 2015, the company has grown rapidly by helping organizations understand what drives successful conversations and deals. With a strong focus on data-driven sales enablement, Gong’s own go-to-market team relies heavily on outbound prospecting to generate qualified leads and book product demos. As a company that preaches the power of AI and analytics, Gong faced an internal challenge: their sales development representatives (SDRs) were sending hundreds of personalized emails each week, yet demo booking rates had stagnated at 2.1%, signaling a need for a more intelligent approach to outreach.

## The Marketing Challenge

Despite having a sophisticated product and a team of skilled SDRs, Gong’s outbound email performance had plateaued. The 2.1% demo booking rate meant that for every 100 personalized emails sent, only about two resulted in a scheduled demo—a figure that was not only inefficient but also costly in terms of time and resources. The core issue was not a lack of effort or personalization; rather, it was the absence of a systematic way to identify which email elements—language, timing, personalization depth, or structure—actually drove conversions. The SDR team relied on intuition and anecdotal best practices, but without data-backed guidance, they could not consistently replicate high-performing outreach. This plateau represented a critical growth bottleneck, as demo bookings are the lifeblood of Gong’s sales pipeline.

## What They Did and Why It Worked

To break through the plateau, Gong’s marketing and sales operations teams built a custom AI model that scored email drafts based on patterns extracted from past successful conversations. The model analyzed thousands of historical emails and linked them to outcomes (demo bookings vs. no response), identifying which linguistic features, subject lines, call-to-action phrases, and personalization tactics correlated with higher conversion rates. The AI then assigned a predictive score to each new email draft, flagging low-scoring messages for revision. SDRs received real-time coaching tips—such as “shorten the subject line” or “add a specific reference to the prospect’s industry trend”—directly within their workflow. This approach worked because it moved beyond generic personalization to data-driven precision. Instead of guessing what might resonate, reps used empirical evidence from Gong’s own conversation data to craft emails that mirrored the language and structure of their most successful peers. The AI also optimized send timing by analyzing when past high-scoring emails were opened and replied to.

## Key Metrics and Results

Within 90 days of implementing the AI-powered email scoring system, Gong’s demo booking rate rose from 2.1% to 3.4%—a 40% increase. This translated into a significant uplift in qualified pipeline without increasing the volume of emails sent. The average email score across the SDR team improved by 28%, and the highest-scoring emails consistently outperformed the lowest-scoring ones by a factor of 2.5x. Additionally, the time SDRs spent on email composition decreased by 15%, as the AI reduced guesswork and revision cycles. The program also boosted SDR morale, as reps felt more confident in their outreach and could see direct feedback on their performance. Beyond the immediate booking lift, Gong’s data science team was able to surface broader insights about prospect preferences, which informed their overall content and messaging strategy.

## 3 Transferable Lessons for Other Marketers

1. **Leverage your own data to train predictive models.** Gong didn’t buy a generic email scoring tool; they built a model using their own historical conversation data. Marketers should look inward first—analyzing past campaign data, CRM records, and customer feedback—to identify patterns that can inform AI training. The most relevant insights often come from your own unique audience.

2. **Integrate AI coaching into existing workflows, not as a separate tool.** The AI scores and tips were embedded directly into the SDR’s email drafting interface, reducing friction and increasing adoption. For any marketing team implementing AI, the key is to make the technology invisible and actionable within the tools your team already uses, rather than requiring them to switch platforms.

3. **Focus on incremental, behavior-driven improvements, not just volume.** Gong’s success came from improving the quality and relevance of each email, not from sending more. Marketers should resist the temptation to scale volume when conversion rates stall. Instead, invest in understanding the specific language, timing, and personalization elements that drive action, and use data to continuously refine those variables.
